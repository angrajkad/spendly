Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 15, 2026

1. Information We Collect

When you create a Spendly account, we collect basic registration information such as your name and email address. As you use the Service, we also collect the expense data you enter — including transaction amounts, categories, dates, and any descriptions you provide. We may additionally collect technical information such as your browser type and IP address for security and diagnostic purposes.

2. How We Use Your Information

Your personal and financial data is used solely to provide and improve the Spendly Service. This includes displaying your expense history, generating spending summaries, and enabling filtering and search features. We may use your email address to send important account-related notifications. We do not use your data for advertising or sell it to any third party.

3. Data Storage & Security

Your data is stored on secured servers with access restricted to authorized systems only. We apply industry-standard security practices including encrypted connections (HTTPS) and hashed password storage. While we take reasonable precautions to protect your information, no internet-based service can guarantee absolute security. You are encouraged to use a strong password and to log out after each session on shared devices.

4. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for commercial purposes. Your expense data is private to your account and is not visible to other users. We may disclose information only if required by law, such as in response to a valid legal order, or to protect the rights and safety of Spendly and its users.

5. Your Rights & Choices

You have the right to access, correct, or delete the personal information associated with your Spendly account at any time. You may export or remove your expense records from within the app. If you wish to permanently delete your account and all associated data, please contact us. We will action your request promptly and in accordance with applicable data protection laws.
